How to Compete

Participating in the FERIT Challenge is straightforward and exciting. Here's how you can get involved:

  • Submit your bot as a single Python (.py) file. The use of external libraries is restricted to numpy. If you require additional libraries or prefer another language, please contact us for support.
  • Once your bot is submitted, it will be entered into an all-vs-all tournament. Each bot will compete against every other bot multiple times to ensure fair results.
  • The leaderboard is based on the number of wins. The more matches your bot wins, the higher you will rank.
Game Rules

The competition is based on the following rules:

  • Two players compete on a 5x5 board.
  • The central field in front of each player is special and represents the goal of the game.
  • Each player has 4 pawns and one king. The king is placed on the central field, and pawns are placed next to it.
  • Each player draws 2 cards, and an additional card is drawn and placed in the center.
  • The cards specify possible moves. Like in chess, where a knight moves in an L shape and a bishop moves diagonally, the cards in Onitama specify how a piece can move. Each move a player makes must be one of the moves shown on one of their two cards.
  • Once a player uses a card, they replace it with the card in the center.
  • Similar to chess, opposing pieces can be captured.
  • Victory is achieved in one of two ways: either by capturing the opponent's king or by moving your king to the opponent's central field.
